About StoryCast

Empowering the next generation of women in technology through authentic stories and accessible content.

Our Mission

StoryCast was created to address a critical gap in the tech industry: the lack of visible role models for girls and women entering technology fields. We believe that representation matters, and hearing from real women who've navigated the journey into tech can inspire and empower the next generation.

Why Audio & Video Storytelling?

We chose audio and video formats because stories become more powerful when you can hear the passion, uncertainty, and triumph in someone's voice. Whether you're commuting, studying, or relaxing, you can access these stories in the format that works best for you.

Our Values

  • Authenticity: Real stories from real people, unfiltered and honest
  • Accessibility: Content that everyone can access, regardless of ability
  • Empowerment: Showing that anyone can learn to code and build tech careers
  • Community: Building connections between women in tech globally

Our Commitment to Accessibility

StoryCast is designed to be accessible to everyone, including people with disabilities. We are committed to meeting WCAG 2.1 Level AA standards and continuously improving our accessibility features.

Acceyssibility Features

Semantic HTML Structure
All pages use proper HTML5 semantic elements (header, main, article, section, nav) to ensure screen readers can navigate the site effectively. Heading hierarchy follows a logical structure from h1 to h6.
Full Transcripts
Every audio episode includes a complete, timestamped transcript. This benefits deaf and hard-of-hearing users, non-native English speakers, and anyone who prefers to read rather than listen.
Video Captions
All videos include closed captions in WebVTT format, synchronized with the audio. Captions can be toggled on or off based on user preference.
Keyboard Navigation
The entire site can be navigated using only a keyboard. All interactive elements have visible focus states, and we include a "skip to main content" link for efficient navigation.
Color Contrast
All text meets WCAG AA contrast ratios (4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large text). We've tested our color combinations to ensure readability for users with low vision or color blindness.
ARIA Labels
We use ARIA (Accessible Rich Internet Applications) attributes where semantic HTML alone is insufficient. This includes aria-label, aria-labelledby, and aria-current to provide additional context to assistive technologies.
Responsive Design
Our site adapts to different screen sizes and zoom levels, ensuring content remains readable and functional at 200% zoom and on mobile devices.
Reduced Motion Support
We respect the prefers-reduced-motion setting for users who experience motion sensitivity or vestibular disorders. Animations are disabled when this preference is detected.

Assistive Technology Compatibility

StoryCast has been tested with the following screen readers and assistive technologies:

  • NVDA (Windows)
  • JAWS (Windows)
  • VoiceOver (macOS and iOS)
  • TalkBack (Android)
