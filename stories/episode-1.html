[00:00] Introduction

Welcome to this panel discussion from the TIME Women of the Year Leadership Forum in Los Angeles. Today, three remarkable women leaders in business discuss the growing role of new technologies, particularly artificial intelligence, in shaping our future.

[00:30] Moderator - Alice Park (TIME Senior Correspondent):

Thank you all for being here. Let's start with a fundamental question: How is AI transforming the way you approach business leadership today?

[01:00] Panel Discussion - The Role of AI in Business

AI has fundamentally changed how we think about decision-making. It's not about replacing human judgment, but augmenting it. We're using AI to analyze patterns we couldn't see before, to predict market trends, and to personalize customer experiences at scale. The key is ensuring we're building these systems with diverse perspectives from the start.

[02:15] On Leveling the Playing Field

What's exciting is how AI can democratize access to powerful tools. Small businesses now have capabilities that were once only available to large corporations. But we must be mindful of ethical implications. As women in tech, we have a responsibility to ensure these technologies are developed inclusively and don't perpetuate existing biases.

[03:45] Addressing the Talent Gap

The talent gap is real. We're seeing unprecedented demand for AI skills, but not enough women entering these fields. That's why mentorship and representation matter. When young women see us in these roles, it changes what they believe is possible for themselves.

[05:00] Advice for Women Interested in Tech

Start where you are. You don't need a computer science degree to contribute to tech. Some of the most important roles in AI development are in ethics, policy, user experience, and business strategy. Find your unique angle and lean into it. Build your networkâ€”communities of women in tech are crucial. The support system makes all the difference.

[07:00] On Taking Action

Don't wait for permission. If you have an idea, pursue it. If you want to learn something, start learning. The barrier to entry has never been lower. Free courses, open-source projects, and communities are eager to help newcomers. Your perspective as a woman brings value that the industry desperately needs.

[08:30] Looking to the Future

There's reason for hope. More women are starting tech companies, reaching C-suite positions, and the conversation has shifted from "why diversity matters" to "how do we achieve it." The next generation is growing up with technology as a natural part of their livesâ€”they're not asking if they belong in tech, they're assuming they do. That confidence is powerful.

[10:30] Systemic Change

We're seeing real progress. Companies are being held accountable for diversity metrics. Investors are asking questions about inclusive hiring. Schools are rethinking how they teach computer science. It's not perfect, but the momentum is building.

[11:15] Closing Remarks

Thank you to our incredible panelists for sharing their insights. The future of technology is being shaped by diverse voices, and these women are leading the way.

[End of panel discussion]
