[02:30] On the African Girls Can Code Initiative (AGCCI)

The African Girls Can Code Initiative is an ambitious project. We are working to bridge the digital divide by training girls from all over the continent in coding, robotics, and design thinking. We want to show them that tech isn't just for a specific group of peopleâ€”it's for every girl who has a dream.

[05:00] Breaking the Myths about Coding

Many girls think you need to be a math genius to code. This is the first myth we break. Coding is about logic, it's about storytelling, and it's about solving problems you see in your community. Once you understand the basics, you realize it's a tool like any other, but one with incredible power to create change.

[08:15] Steps to Start Your Tech Journey

If you're a girl listening right now and you want to start: first, don't be afraid to fail. Your first line of code will probably throw an error, and that's okay. Second, find a community. Whether it's an AGCCI camp or an online group, having peers who are also learning makes the journey so much better. And third, identify a small problem and try to solve it using tech.

[11:00] The Role of Mentorship

We need more role models. When a girl sees a woman leading a tech firm or developing a life-saving app, she thinks "I can do that too." That's why we invite successful women developers to speak to our students. Mentorship provides that bridge between learning and professional success.

[13:45] Final Thoughts for Every Girl

The future is digital. By learning to code, you're not just getting a job skillâ€”you're getting a seat at the table where the future is being decided. Every girl in Africa has the potential to be a tech leader. Start today, start small, but just start.
